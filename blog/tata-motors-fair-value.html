Simple way to estimate Tata Motors' fair value

Tata Motors is a cyclical company — earnings can swing. That means you must be extra conservative with growth and margin of safety. Here is a compact approach anyone can follow.

Step 1 — Find current EPS

Use a reliable source. If trailing EPS is volatile, consider a 3-year average.

Step 2 — Use a conservative growth rate

For cyclical firms, use a lower growth number (3–8%) unless there’s clear evidence of sustained expansion.

Step 3 — Forward EPS & Fair P/E

Calculate forward EPS = EPS × (1 + growth). Then pick a fair P/E: conservative cap = 12–15 for weak/uncertain growth.

Step 4 — Safety first

Apply a higher margin of safety (30–40%) for cyclical businesses to account for earnings drops.
