How to find a stock's intrinsic value — quick guide

Intrinsic value is an estimate of what a stock should be worth based on earnings and reasonable assumptions. This quick guide shows the exact steps so you can do it yourself or use our tool.

Checklist of inputs

  • Current EPS (trailing 12 months)
  • Expected EPS growth (next 12 months)
  • Industry or historical P/E
  • Market price (for comparison)

3-minute method

  1. Get EPS (TTM).
  2. Choose a realistic growth (don’t overestimate).
  3. Compute forward EPS: EPS × (1+growth).
  4. Select a fair P/E (use industry or historical range, cap it conservatively).
  5. Intrinsic value = forward EPS × fair P/E. Apply margin of safety (25–30%).

This method is simple and fast — great for screening. For final buy decisions, always verify fundamentals: revenue trends, cash flow, and debt levels.
