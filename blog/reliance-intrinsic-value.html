How to estimate Reliance Industries' intrinsic value (simple)

This short guide shows a clear, step-by-step way to estimate intrinsic value for Reliance Industries (or any large Indian stock) using easy inputs: EPS, a reasonable growth rate, and a fair P/E.

Step 1 — Get EPS (TTM)

EPS (trailing) is the company's earnings per share over the last 12 months. You can find this on financial sites or use the valuation tool. If EPS = 30 (example), we use that as the starting point.

Step 2 — Choose a realistic growth

Be conservative. For large companies, 8–12% annual growth is a reasonable starting point. If growth is uncertain, pick the lower end.

Step 3 — Compute forward EPS

Forward EPS = EPS × (1 + growth). Example: 30 × 1.10 = 33.

Step 4 — Choose a fair P/E

Use industry P/E or historical company P/E as guide. Cap the P/E if growth is low. For most mature firms, a fair P/E can be 12–18 depending on consistency of growth.

Step 5 — Intrinsic value

Intrinsic Value = Forward EPS × Fair P/E. Apply a margin of safety (25–30%) before deciding to buy.
